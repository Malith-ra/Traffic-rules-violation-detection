Literature Survey

A. Vehicle Type And Number Plate Detection

Automatic Number Plate Detection technology has made its way into many areas of today's digital environment thanks to the capacity to identify and scan huge quantities of moving cars [2][3]. While the Automatic Number Plate Detection technology may be supplied in many various forms, they all have the same fundamental purpose to offer a very precise vehicle scanning system without any human involvement [2]. Initially, the most essential and crucial step for this kind of system was the successful extraction of the image/video number plate [4][5]. The extraction rate is a measure at which plates have been successfully extracted, with the entire number of input pictures or vehicles identified. The number plate can be darkened or damaged with dirt or situated beyond the camera's sight (because various cars are placed in different locations at different places on the body of the vehicle) [2]. Algorithms that use geometric characteristics to extract rectangle-formed numbers may have problems if many similar forms are drawn/pasted across the vehicle body. Additional algorithms must also be employed to remove undesirable areas, along with rectangle-shaped features. Number Plate Recognition means that pictures from the desired scene are acquired using a camera on the number plate [3]. An alpha-numeric translation of recorded pictures into a text input is captured by still photos or photographic video and then processed via a series of recognitional algorithms based on photo processing. [3].

The video surveillance systems are typically intended for operators to monitor protected space remotely or to capture video data as a subsequent analytical archive at a time. Video surveillance is a labor demanding job for controlling a big number of cameras [4]. It is also a laborious job and human observers may quickly lose sight of it. Automation may assist to solve costs and performance problems [5]. It may also release security workers from mundane duties and allow them to concentrate on cognitive work on a higher level that makes greater use of their skills. Intelligent video surveillance in industrial applications is of significant interest since the need for reducing the workforce is increasing.

B. Parking Violation Detection and Lane Violation Detection

The ever-increasing number of violations of traffic rules has become a critical challenge in Sri Lanka. The solution is to install violation detection systems on the expressway. It is an essential part of road security and safety. There is a huge increase with the use of vehicles on the expressway. Consequently, there is an increase in violations of traffic rules. Such violations lead to more road accidents.

A real problem related to traffic jams and highway accidents. This may occur due to limited allocation of the traffic police to the expressway. The system acts as an individual system without any assistance from other external systems, such as the sensors. The system can detect violations of vehicle parking and drive wrong lane. The system automatically detects traffic rules violated vehicles by methods of video processing techniques.

Every other videos of camera-based violations tracking system that can operate automated event detection usually includes multi-stage video analysis sequences for CCTV cameras from expressways. The effective detection of illegal parking has captivated research attention from different domains. Researchers are working to develop new algorithms aimed at improving detection accuracy. The majority of these efforts have used videos taken from fixed cameras . Overcoming this issue of the coverage of fixed cameras, recent research have applied alternate solution data sources, including certain biking trajectories, to identify illegal parking. Our research improves on these past researches by using CCTV cameras that could be implemented in object detection.

C. Vehicle Speed Detection

Many researchers have done the system implementation using image and video processing techniques. Some of the authors use videos that are taken from surveillance cameras as inputs.

According to , the system is done in three parts. The first part which is the vehicle detection part they have used background subtraction. Here a new method called adaptive background subtraction is used. In adaptive background subtraction, the background image is updating itself and it handles any changes of illuminations. Next for the vehicle tracking, they have separated this task into two parts namely, object segmentation and object labeling, bounding box, and center extraction. In object labeling, first, they have done shadow removal to prevent false detections using some of the properties of a shadow. Then, Convex hull and some other morphological operations are carried out and as the result, the noise removed images that have vehicles as proper blobs are obtained. The second part of the tracking is to track the objects that were detected. In here a unique label is assigned to the vehicle and keep it until it disappears from the scene. And then a bounding box is drawn around the vehicle and the center of the box is calculated and preserved. The final phase which is the speed detection part is separated into two parts. The first part is finding a calibration factor. In here it was done by calibrating the camera. And the second part is speed calculation. Here the speed in km/h is calculated and that speed is updating every 0.5sec.

According to , the vehicle speed estimation is divided into four parts. The first part is background reconstruction and update strategy. Here the median method in RGB space is used in background reconstruction and for the background updating process, a mathematical expression is used. The second part is to extract the moving vehicle. For this process, they have combined the three-frame difference method and background difference method and this process is conducted in gray space. The third phase is to obtain the position of the vehicle centroid. This phase is done using a formula created by them. For the last step, which is calculating the speed of the vehicle, they must use a specific formula. Here the camera calibration method is used to map the pixel distance with actual distance.

According to , the system implantation is divided into three parts. The first part is object detection. For this, the Gaussian mixture model is used for background subtraction, and for specifying the segments of foreground points DBSCAN clustering is used. Then bounding boxes are used to mark these segments. In the object tracking phase which is the next step they have used Kalman filter and Optical flow methods are used. Then for the last step which is speed estimation weight of the pixel is needed to know. For this, they have divided the width of the road by the pixel representation. Then by calculating the average speed of all the points, the speed of the target is calculated.

C. Vehicle Speed Detection

• Input videos obtained through CCTV cameras placed in the highways.

• Then the frames are preprocessed and resized using OpenCV functions.

• After, frames are converted to its grayscale equivalent using OpenCV functions.

• Haar Classifier with Adaboost technique is used for vehicle detection.

• The correlation tracker function of the dlib library is used to track the vehicles in the video.

• From above phases position of the vehicle is obtained in terms of pixel units.

• In calculating speed, initially the change in pixels is calculated using coordinates obtained.

• Then the distance in terms of pixel units is converted into the SI units of distance.

• The time elapsed is obtained by camera’s frame rate settings (fps value).

• Then the speed is calculated by multiplying distance by fps value and then by 3.6 which is used to convert the speed into kilometers per hours.

D. Reverse Violation Detection

• CCTV cameras captures the real time moving vehicles from the Express way.

• The frames are divided into single size from captured Data.

• The frames are preprocessed and resized using OpenCV functions.

• Then the next step is to load the video and convert it into gray-scale.

• TensorFlow is used to detects, locating and tracing a vehicle from a video.

• YOLO algorithm is used to get high accuracy and speed of object detection in real-time.

• In the reverse violation, first system check if vehicle is driving on wrong direction, then system detect that object.

• Then individual frames are point into the specific object and display this is a reverse violation.

• In the overtake violation, when one object is overtaking another object, It will be detected first using YOLO.

• After that set the annotations to the overtaking vehicle object and check whether lights are on/off. If light is off within specific time duration, It will be detecting and give output as an overtaking violation, Otherwise usually system run its own way.
